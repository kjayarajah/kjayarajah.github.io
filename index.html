I'm recruiting PhD students to work with me, drop me a note if you're interested in mobile computing, IoT and autonomous systems!

I'm an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at New Jersey Institute of Technology. My core research interests lie in the areas of mobile and ubiquitous sensing, deep learning for IoT and the edge, mobility-driven urban analytics and predictive cyber-physical systems.

In my most recent work, I’m exploring the concept of "Collaborative AI-based sense-making" for resource-limited edge devices (e.g., infrastructural cameras, autonomous systems, wearable computers, etc.) where groups of sensors, due to their proximity and shared perspectives of the sensed environment, can cooperate for performance benefits. Previously, I explored the use of localization technology in improving the operations of built environments, both at the indoor-scale and city scale. I have worked on a variety of predictive urban applications (e.g., predicting non-conformant behavior, mobility-on-demand simulations/public transportation, consumer marketing and retail survivability), building on both social and behavioral insights derived from individual and group mobility.
